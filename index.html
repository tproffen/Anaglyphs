What are anaglyphs?

We see the world in 3D, because each eye sees a slightly different picture. The two images are then processes by our brain to produce our 3D view of the world.

Anaglyphs use filters, to separate two different images encoded in a picture or movie to create the same 3D effect. Glasses like the ones shown on the right, allow separating the two parts of an stereoscopic image by color in a red and cyan part for each eye. Try the tools below to draw in 3D or take pictures in 3D right using two webcams right from the browser.

 
Looking for glasses - check Amazon.com.

Drawing Pad

Click the Start program button on the right to open a window running the drawing program. Use the mouse to draw lines. Explore the depth slider, and see how the lines appear above or below the plane of the computer screen. Look at the image with and without the glasses. What changes as you change the depth? Once done, right click on the image to save it. Share with your friends or post on social media like any other image.

 

Anaglyph Webcam

Taking a picture in 3D requires two webcams attached to the computer. Click the Start program button on the right to open a window running the program. The webcams should be separated by a typical eye separation distance and point in the same direction. Some adjustment to the camera position might be needed for best results. Click Take picture to snap a photo. Once done, right click on the image to save it.
